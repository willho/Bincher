Yes! Self-improving pattern detection. Pincher teaches the backend:

Flow
Pincher notices during batch analysis:
"Volume spike + whale entry within 5 min = pump incoming"
Pincher creates backend rule:
→ "When volume 3x AND whale buys within 5 min, trigger my analysis"

Only calls AI when pattern matches (rare, valuable)
How It Works
Pincher-generated backend triggers:

pattern_triggers
├── id
├── name ("volume_whale_combo")
├── conditions [
│     { type: "volume_spike", threshold: 3x },
│     { type: "whale_buy", within: "5min" }
│   ]
├── action ("trigger_ai_analysis" | "send_alert" | "both")
├── created_by ("pincher")
├── hit_count (tracks usefulness)
├── last_triggered

Smart. Smooth pacing, not cliff edges:

Budget Pacing Model
Daily budget: $1.00
Current time: 2pm (58% of day gone)
Spent so far: $0.70 (70% of budget)
Status: Running hot (+12% over pace)
Action: Gentle slowdown, not panic
Dynamic Throttling
Pace vs Budget	Adjustment
On track (±10%)	Normal speed
Slightly hot (+10-20%)	Stretch batch interval 15→20 min
Running hot (+20-30%)	Stretch to 25 min, skip low-heat tokens
Burning (+30%+)	30 min batches, priority tokens only
Underspent (-20%+)	Speed up, more analysis
Gradual, Not Sudden
Hour 1:  Batch every 15 min (normal)
Hour 3:  Spent 40% of budget, 25% of day gone
         → Stretch to 18 min
Hour 6:  Spent 55% of budget, 50% of day gone  
         → Back to 15 min (on pace now)
Hour 10: Spent 85% of budget, 80% of day gone
         → Stay at 15 min, slight buffer
Hour 12: 5% budget left
         → Priority tokens only, 30 min intervals
What Pincher Tells User
Pincher: "Running a bit hot today - lots of action. 
I'm stretching my check intervals a little so I 
last till midnight. Your priority tokens still 
get the good stuff."
No sudden "I'm offline" - just graceful slowdown that user barely notices.
