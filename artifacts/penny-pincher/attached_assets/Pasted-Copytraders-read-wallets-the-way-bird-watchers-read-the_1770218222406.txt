Copytraders read wallets the way bird-watchers read the sky. Not one omen, but a pattern of flight 🦅📊
Here are the main signals they actually watch, grouped by how serious they are.


---

1. Wallet quality signals (who to follow)

Before a single trade is copied, the wallet itself is judged.

Core indicators

Win rate. Not raw percent, but consistency across many trades.

Realised PnL. Closed profit only. Paper gains lie.

Trade count. 5 wins is luck. 200 trades is a fingerprint.

Time-in-market. Holds long enough to dodge MEV churn, short enough to exit hype.


Advanced tells

Buys before volume spikes, not during.

Partial sells on the way up, not full rage exits.

Survives multiple market regimes, not just one meme season.


Red flags 🚩

One massive win carrying the stats.

Repeated round-trip losses on low liquidity pairs.

Always first in but never first out.



---

2. Entry signals (when they buy)

This is where most copytraders bleed.

On-chain

Fresh buy after a deploy wallet but before socials light up.

Multiple smart wallets entering within a narrow time window.

Size discipline. Entry is meaningful, not dust, not YOLO.


Market

Liquidity added before the buy, not after.

Slippage tolerance tight. Sloppy slippage screams panic.

Price still near local VWAP, not vertical.



---

3. Exit signals (when they sell)

Exits matter more than entries.

Partial sells at predefined multiples.

Sell clustering. Smart wallets quietly leaving together.

First sell before trend breaks, not after.


Red flags

No sells ever. That’s not conviction, that’s denial.

Full exit at first green candle every time.



---

4. Risk & sizing behavior

Copytraders love boring discipline.

Position size scales with conviction, not mood.

Losses capped. No “double down because vibes”.

Gas paid deliberately. No frantic spam.



---

5. Behavioral fingerprints (hard to fake)

These separate humans from bots and lucky gamblers.

Repeats the same playbook across tokens.

Reacts rationally to bad fills.

Stops trading during chaos instead of forcing entries.



---

6. Meta signals (copytrade-specific)

What you look for if you’re running or choosing a copy bot.

Latency sensitivity. If edge disappears in 2 blocks, copying is pointless.

Crowding risk. Wallets already being copied lose alpha fast.

Copy survivability. Strategy still works after fees + slippage.



---

Copytraders aren’t chasing green candles.
They’re chasing repeatable behavior under pressure.
